A Brief History

The use of beauty and personal care products is not a modern invention. Evidence of their use dates back thousands of years:

  • Ancient Civilizations: Ancient Egyptians used scented oils for hygiene and to protect their skin from the sun. They also used kohl as eyeliner. Ancient Greeks and Romans used powdered chalk and other substances to create a pale complexion, which was a sign of wealth.

  • Middle Ages and Renaissance: While the use of makeup was often associated with moral concerns, the upper classes continued to use various products. The use of elaborate wigs, powders, and fragrances was common.

  • Modern Era: The 20th century saw the mass production of cosmetics and the rise of major brands. The concept of a "beauty routine" became widespread, and products became more accessible to everyone. Scientific advancements in dermatology and chemistry led to a greater understanding of ingredients and product efficacy.
